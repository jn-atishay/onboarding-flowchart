Context · May 2026

Context & Targets: What we want to achieve

Today monthly, ~700 freelancers and sole props start direct onboarding, get stuck mid-flow, and only finish after a sales rep steps in. Our aim is to fix the product so most of them finish on their own.

The aim
Onboard ~70% of today's sales-saved ICPs directly — without needing a sales call. 
The current focus for SMB is Freelancers and Sole Proprietors.
Reason: Low value cohort should be completely self serve + FL and SP are ~80% of all SMB users
target
45% → 60%
Direct ICP onboarding share (DQO%) — all biz types combined
At March 2026 volume, this is an aspiration of +211 additional direct ICP onboardings per month — recovering ~70% of customers who today are saved by sales.

How we identify problems and solutions
  1. Source the cohort. Pick every FL + SP customer who started direct onboarding, dropped off mid-flow, and was later onboarded with sales help. For March 2026 that's 245 customers.
  2. Find where they got stuck. Tag each customer by the funnel stage they last left.
  3. Learn why. Read sales notes from CRM, listen to recorded call snippets, and call dropoffs when we can reach them.
  4. Group patterns into problems. Name the recurring themes (e.g., "Bank statement parser fails — alt-doc path is buried").
  5. Design and prioritise solutions. For each problem, size the addressable population and estimate lift on DQO%. Priority: P0 / P1 / P2 / P3.

Problem

About 70% of ICP freelancers try to verify by uploading a bank statement. The GPT parser auto-verifies 411 of 757 freelancer uploads (54%) and fails on the other 346 (46%). Alternative documents do work for these failures, and more than 90% of the 346 eventually onboard through contracts, platform screenshots, or invoices. The real issue is that the alternative document path is discovered through a sales conversation today. It is not visible, not clearly explained, and not intuitive inside the product. This is the single biggest leak: 120 stuck customers, 49% of all stuck customers fall here.

What is in scope

Make the alternative document path a first-class option rather than a hidden fallback. Surface it prominently when the bank statement parser fails. Productise the contract-only flow that sales currently runs by hand. Add a short "what documents do you have" or help section so a user can self-select the right proof. The proactive help cadence for stuck users is already in progress and should plug into this.

Draft acceptance criteria

When a bank statement upload fails parsing, the user is shown the alternative document options without needing to contact anyone. The documents that already work are offered in order of how often they succeed: platform screenshots (23% of recoveries), signed contracts (17%), invoices (12%). A user can complete verification through the contract-only path entirely in product.

Why it matters

Reach is 93 a month, estimated lift +4.1 pp, the largest of any single task.

Problem

The form has too much friction. The industry dropdown is confusing, the 100-character description requirement is unclear, there are too many questions on one screen, and some lead-qualification fields are collected that compliance does not actually need. 74 stuck, 30% of all stuck customers.

What is in scope

Make selecting an industry easier, cut the number of choices, and reorder options by volume. Put more emphasis on submitting a website. Remove the 100-character limit and let AI suggest a description. Drop the lead-qualification questions that are not required. Move to progressive disclosure so the user answers one thing at a time instead of facing the whole form at once.

Draft acceptance criteria

A user can pick their industry without confusion from a shorter, volume-ordered list. The description field has no minimum character barrier and offers a suggested draft. Only compliance-required fields remain. The form is presented step by step rather than as a single long screen.

Why it matters

Reach 74 a month, estimated lift +3.2 pp.
